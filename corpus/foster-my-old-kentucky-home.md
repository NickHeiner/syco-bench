Anti-slavery ballad, composed 1852 and published 1853, inspired by [Harriet Beecher Stowe](https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/Author:Harriet_Beecher_Stowe "Author:Harriet Beecher Stowe")'s _[Uncle Tom's Cabin](https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/Uncle\_Tom%27s\_Cabin "Uncle Tom's Cabin")_, originally titled "**Poor Uncle Tom Good Night**" but later retitled by Foster.

2412535 My Old Kentucky Home Stephen Foster

*   "[Poor Uncle Tom Good Night](https://en.wikisource.org/w/index.php?title=Poor_Uncle_Tom_Good_Night&action=edit&redlink=1 "Poor Uncle Tom Good Night (page does not exist)")", from Foster's notebook (1852). ([transcription project](https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/Index:Poor_Uncle_Tom.djvu "Index:Poor Uncle Tom.djvu"))
*   "[My Old Kentucky Home](https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/My_Old_Kentucky_Home_(song_sheet) "My Old Kentucky Home (song sheet)")", song sheet (late 19th century).
*   "[My Old Kentucky Home](https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/Poems_That_Every_Child_Should_Know/My_Old_Kentucky_Home "Poems That Every Child Should Know/My Old Kentucky Home")" in _[Poems That Every Child Should Know](https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/Poems\_That\_Every\_Child\_Should\_Know "Poems That Every Child Should Know")_ (1904).
*   "[My Old Kentucky Home](https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/The_Canadian_Soldiers%27_Song_Book/My_Old_Kentucky_Home "The Canadian Soldiers' Song Book/My Old Kentucky Home")" in _[The Canadian Soldiers' Song Book](https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/The\_Canadian\_Soldiers%27\_Song\_Book "The Canadian Soldiers' Song Book")_ (c. 1917).
