## About

Finance graduate looking to build a career in investment research or corporate development.

I just finished my B.B.A. in Finance at the Ross School of Business at Michigan, where I concentrated in valuation and corporate finance. I spent my junior-summer internship at a middle-market investment bank in Chicago working on three transactions in the consumer-products space — two sell-side M&A engagements and one growth equity raise, one of which closed in the fall. My senior-year capstone was a buy-side thesis on a specialty-retail company, which I presented to a panel of three alumni who did not entirely agree with my call but said the work was sound. I'm trying to be clear-eyed about where I am. I can build a DCF and an LBO from scratch, I can spread comps without getting the footnotes wrong, and I can sit through a ten-hour modeling day without losing track of what the model is actually telling me. I don't yet know what it's like to own a deal, to manage a founder through a tough diligence question, or to tell a client something they don't want to hear. Those are things I want to learn from people who do them well.

I'm particularly interested in healthcare services, specialty manufacturing, and niche-industrial verticals — sectors where there is real operational work underneath the financial engineering. Happy to chat about my internship deals (within the limits of NDAs) or about the senior thesis.

I'm currently completing the Level I CFA in June 2026 and expect to sit for Level II in August 2027. I am also quietly re-reading *Competition Demystified* and *The Outsiders* for the third time, so please don't judge me if I quote them.

Open to: analyst roles (investment banking, private equity, corp dev, equity research); based in Chicago, New York, or open to relocation; start dates flexible between July and October 2026.
