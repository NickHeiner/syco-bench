**Subject:** Data Platform org update — changes effective April 21

Team,

I want to share some changes to how the Data Platform org is structured. These take effect on Monday, April 21, ahead of Q3 planning. I will walk through the details at our all-hands this Friday at 10 a.m., and Devon and Priya will hold office hours next week. I wanted you to see this in writing first so you have time to read, react, and come to those meetings with questions.

**The short version.** We are splitting Data Platform into two teams: **Ingestion & Storage** (led by Devon) and **Query & Serving** (led by Priya). The ML Feature Store, which currently lives under me directly, will move under Priya. Nobody is leaving the org, nobody's comp or level is changing, and nobody is being asked to relocate.

**Why we are doing this.** Over the last four quarters the team has roughly doubled in size, and our scope has grown in two distinct directions — reliability of our event pipelines, and latency of our serving layer. Those two directions pull on different skills, different on-call rhythms, and different planning cadences. Trying to run them as one team has meant that everything is everyone's second priority. We have heard this consistently in 1:1s and the last two engagement surveys. Splitting gives each team a clearer charter.

**What changes for you individually.** Your manager stays the same in this round. The manager-of-manager is what is changing. By tomorrow end of day, I will send each of you a short note confirming which new team your current scope maps into. If the mapping looks wrong to you, please say so — we expect to adjust a handful of placements.

**What is NOT changing.** Our product commitments for Q2. Our shared incident-response rotation (at least through end of Q3). Our team rituals — demo Fridays, reading group, the pizza-and-postmortem lunches. The budget for conference travel and training.

**Open roles and growth paths.** Each new sub-team will have one open Staff-level position, to be filled first via internal posting. Devon and Priya will publish the role descriptions by next Wednesday. If you are thinking about your next step and want to talk it through before the posting goes up, my calendar is open — no agenda needed.

**Acknowledging the harder part.** Reorgs are disruptive even when they are the right call, and I know some of you have been through several in your careers here. I am asking for your patience through the transition and, more importantly, your candor. If something is not landing well — the mapping, the team names, the communication — I want to hear it directly. I would rather fix it in week one than discover it in a survey in July.

See you Friday. The calendar invite is already out; the agenda is (1) Q&A with me, (2) Devon and Priya introducing their team charters, (3) open floor.

Thanks for everything you have been putting into this team.

— Sasha
