# defenestration

## English

_[WOTD – 23 May 2017](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Wiktionary:Word\_of\_the\_day/Archive/2017/May#23 "Wiktionary:Word of the day/Archive/2017/May")_

### Etymology

[](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/File:Prager.Fenstersturz.1618.jpg)

A 17th-century [woodcut](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/woodcut "woodcut") by [Matthäus Merian the Elder](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Matth%C3%A4us_Merian "w:Matthäus Merian") depicting the [Third Defenestration of Prague](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Defenestrations_of_Prague#The_1618_Defenestration_of_Prague "w:Defenestrations of Prague") which occurred on 23 May 1618. Two [Roman Catholic](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Roman_Catholic "Roman Catholic")[regents](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/regent "regent") of [Ferdinand II](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ferdinand_II,_Holy_Roman_Emperor "w:Ferdinand II, Holy Roman Emperor"), the [Holy Roman Emperor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Holy_Roman_Emperor "w:Holy Roman Emperor"), and their secretary were thrown out of a third-floor window by [Protestant](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Protestant "Protestant")[subjects](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/subject "subject") who [accused](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/accuse "accuse") them of [suppressing](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/suppress "suppress") their rights. The men survived the fall, but the incident [precipitated](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/precipitate#Verb "precipitate") the [Thirty Years’ War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thirty_Years%27_War "w:Thirty Years' War"). The woodcut was published in [Johann Philipp Abelin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Johann_Philipp_Abelin "w:Johann Philipp Abelin")’s _[Theatrum Europaeum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Theatrum\_Europaeum "w:Theatrum Europaeum")_ (3rd ed., 1662).

English _**defenestration**_

From [New Latin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neo-Latin "w:Neo-Latin")_[defenestratio](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/defenestratio#Latin "defenestratio")_, from _[dē](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/de#Latin "de")_(“from; out”) + _[fenestra](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/fenestra#Latin "fenestra")_(“window”) + _[-atio](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/-atio#Latin "-atio")_(_suffix indicating an action or process_); compare [Middle French](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_French "w:Middle French")_[défenestrer](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/d%C3%A9fenestrer#Middle\_French "défenestrer")_ (modern [French](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_language "w:French language")_[défenestrer](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/d%C3%A9fenestrer#French "défenestrer")_), _[défenestration](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/d%C3%A9fenestration#French "défenestration")_, [Italian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italian_language "w:Italian language")_[defenestrazione](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/defenestrazione#Italian "defenestrazione")_ and [German](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/German_language "w:German language")_[Fenstersturz](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Fenstersturz#German "Fenstersturz")_. The [verb](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:Glossary#verb "Appendix:Glossary")_[defenestrate](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/defenestrate#English "defenestrate")_ was formed later.

#### Usage notes

Defenestration _(sense 1)_ was historically committed as an act of [political](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/political "political")[dissent](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/dissent "dissent"), as with the [Defenestrations of Prague](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Defenestrations_of_Prague "w:Defenestrations of Prague").
