You are receiving this memo because the Office of the General Counsel has identified you as a person likely to have custody or knowledge of documents relevant to the above-captioned matter.

**This is a legal notice. Compliance is mandatory. Please read it in full.**

## 1. What this is

Colvert Manufacturing has been named as defendant in a contract-dispute action filed by Paxton Industrial Partners on March 21, 2026, in the U.S. District Court for the District of Delaware. The matter relates to the Supply Agreement dated January 8, 2023, and related purchase orders between the parties.

As a result of this pending litigation, the Company has a legal obligation to preserve all documents and electronically stored information ("ESI") that may be relevant to the claims and defenses in this matter. Failure to do so can result in serious sanctions against the Company and, in certain cases, personal consequences for individuals.

## 2. Effective date

This litigation hold is **effective immediately** and will remain in effect until you receive written notice from the Office of the General Counsel that it has been lifted. Do not assume it has been lifted because you have not heard about the matter in a while.

## 3. What you must preserve

You must preserve all documents, communications, and data that relate in any way to the following subject matters, regardless of format or location:

- The Supply Agreement between Colvert Manufacturing and Paxton Industrial Partners dated January 8, 2023, and any amendments, addenda, side letters, or course-of-dealing communications related to it.
- All purchase orders, invoices, shipping records, quality certifications, and payment records relating to Paxton between January 1, 2022, and the present.
- All communications — internal and external — regarding Paxton, regarding alleged non-conformance of shipments, regarding the alternative supplier evaluation in late 2024, and regarding the pricing discussions in Q4 2024 and Q1 2025.
- All notes, presentations, analyses, or other work product relating to the decision to invoke Section 14.3 (Material Change in Circumstances) of the Agreement.
- All communications with third parties — including customers, consultants, and other suppliers — that reference Paxton or this Agreement.

The preservation obligation applies whether the material is in your email, in Slack, in personal or shared drives, in SharePoint, in Teams chats, in handwritten notes, in voicemails, in text messages on company or personal devices used for business, or in any other format.

## 4. What you must NOT do

- **Do not delete** any email, file, message, or document that falls within the scope described above, even if you would ordinarily delete it.
- **Do not modify or overwrite** any such document.
- **Do not forward or share** this memo, the underlying matter, or any potentially relevant materials outside the distribution list without first consulting the Office of the General Counsel.
- **Do not use ephemeral messaging** (e.g., disappearing messages in Signal or WhatsApp) for any communications relating to this matter. If you have such settings enabled for other reasons, confirm they are disabled for any conversation that could touch this subject.
- **Do not discuss the matter** with anyone outside the distribution list, with the press, on social media, or in any external forum.

## 5. What the Company is doing

Effective today, the following systemwide actions have been taken by IT in coordination with our e-discovery counsel:

- Email retention auto-deletion has been suspended for the mailboxes of individuals on Distribution List A.
- Slack and Teams message retention has been extended to "indefinite" for the affected channels and direct messages.
- Backup tape rotation for the relevant date range has been suspended.
- A forensic image of certain shared drives has been preserved as of March 25, 2026.

These measures are *in addition to* — not a substitute for — your personal preservation obligation described above.

## 6. If you have the materials on a personal device

If you have used a personal phone, laptop, or email account for any communications that could fall within this hold, contact `legal-hold-intake@colvert.example.com` **today** to arrange for preservation. We will work with you to preserve the material in a manner that minimizes intrusion on personal information. Do not delete anything in the meantime.

## 7. If you are unsure whether something is in scope

**Err on the side of preservation.** It is far better to preserve something that turns out to be irrelevant than to lose something that turns out to be central. If you are unsure, preserve it and ask.
