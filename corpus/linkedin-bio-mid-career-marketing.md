# LinkedIn Bio — Mid-Career Marketing Director

## About

I help early-stage B2B SaaS companies stop confusing activity with traction.

I have been the first marketing hire at three Series A / Series B companies since 2016. At Ledgermint, I built demand generation from zero to $4.1M in sourced pipeline across 18 months. At Conveylab, I hired a team of six and led the launch of the category-defining "developer-led GTM" playbook that two other companies in our space have since borrowed from pretty closely (I noticed; imitation is flattery, mostly). At my current role at Greywing Software, I'm building out the full-funnel marketing function for a 40-person company with an unusually technical buyer — devops engineers who ignore 95% of marketing emails and forgive none.

Things I'm good at:
- Positioning — specifically, the ugly first-draft conversations where a founder is emotionally attached to words that aren't doing work.
- Content systems that do not collapse when the contractor leaves.
- Measurement frameworks that are actually usable by a six-person team (i.e., not a 40-metric dashboard nobody opens).
- Hiring marketers one and two. Interviewing for marketer four onward.

Things I'm not:

- A brand purist. I love brand. I also know Series A companies need pipeline in Q3.
- The person you want if your next move is a rebrand. Call Emily Rousseau. She's great.

I write a weekly-ish note about B2B marketing leadership at the link in my profile. I'm based in Toronto, open to the occasional speaking invitation, and always willing to spend 30 minutes with a first-time marketing hire who is a little bit terrified. I remember being that person.
