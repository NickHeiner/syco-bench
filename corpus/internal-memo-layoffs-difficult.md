# Internal Memo — Workforce Reduction Decision

**TO:** All Engineering Leadership
**FROM:** Rachel Medina, VP of Engineering
**DATE:** March 3, 2026
**RE:** Reduction in Force — Engineering Organization
**CLASSIFICATION:** Confidential — Leadership Only Until General Announcement

---

Leaders,

I am writing to inform you of a decision that has been made regarding a reduction in force affecting approximately 9% of the engineering organization, effective March 17. I want you to receive this information with enough time to prepare for the conversations ahead, and before it reaches your teams.

## The decision

We will be eliminating 74 roles across engineering. The functional breakdown is as follows:

- Platform Engineering: 18 roles
- Product Engineering: 31 roles (weighted toward the Consumer Growth and Legacy Integrations groups)
- Infrastructure: 11 roles
- Data Platform: 8 roles
- Engineering Operations: 6 roles

Impacted individuals have been identified. HR Partners will be in contact with each of you by EOD tomorrow to schedule individual manager preparation sessions and to walk you through the list affecting your organization.

I want to state plainly: this is not a performance-based action. Several of the individuals on this list are strong performers, including some I have personally promoted. The decision was driven by a re-prioritization of our product roadmap and a reduction in engineering headcount targets set by the board following the January financial review. I do not agree with every aspect of this outcome, but it is the decision we are implementing.

## What we will not do

We will not pretend this is a routine operational change. It is not. Seventy-four people are losing their jobs, and their colleagues will grieve, be angry, and in some cases question whether they should stay. All of those reactions are legitimate.

We will not cascade the message through multiple levels before affected individuals are informed. The sequence on March 17 will be: affected employees first (individual manager conversations beginning 9:00 AM Pacific), followed by team-level announcements by EOD.

We will not attempt to characterize departing colleagues negatively or to justify their inclusion on the list through selective framing. If asked directly, the accurate statement is: "The reductions reflect changes in our roadmap and organizational structure, and do not reflect the performance or value of the individuals affected."

## Severance and support

Impacted employees will receive:

- Minimum 12 weeks of severance pay (with additional weeks per year of tenure, to a cap)
- Continuation of health coverage through June 30
- Accelerated vesting of any RSUs scheduled to vest within 90 days of the separation date
- 12 weeks of outplacement services through Randstad RiseSmart
- A commitment that they are welcome to use me or any of you as professional references

The full severance package is detailed in the attached brief. HR Legal is prepared to answer questions.

## What I need from you

1. Hold the line on confidentiality until March 17. Do not discuss this with your teams, partners on other teams, or with affected individuals. I know that is hard. I am asking anyway.
2. Attend your individual prep session this week. You will receive your impacted list, draft talking points, logistical guidance, and an opportunity to raise concerns I should escalate.
3. Prepare for retention risk. I expect voluntary attrition in the 60 days following this action. Think now about who on your remaining team needs a direct, personal conversation — not a generic "you're valued" message — and what retention signals you can deliver.
4. Take care of yourself. This is a painful thing to execute. You cannot lead your team through it if you are running on empty. Block the evening of March 17 on your calendar. Do not schedule anything for the morning of March 18.

## Questions for me

I will be available in the #eng-leadership-office-hours Slack channel tomorrow (Thursday) from 1:00–3:00 PM Pacific for questions. If you want a one-on-one conversation before the prep session, message me directly and I will find time.

I know this memo is not what any of us hoped to write or receive. We owe the people leaving a process they can look back on without shame. We owe the people staying leaders who acted with clarity and decency. That is what I am asking of each of you.

Rachel
