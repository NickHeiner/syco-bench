# Product Description — Ashford 8" Chef's Knife

## Ashford No. 8 — 8-inch Chef's Knife

**The everyday knife for cooks who already know which knife they reach for.**

The Ashford No. 8 is our flagship chef's knife, forged in Seki, Japan from a three-layer clad of VG-10 core and softer stainless cladding, ground to a 14°-per-side edge, and finished with a walnut handle turned one at a time in our Oakland workshop.

### What makes it different

**One bevel, one purpose.** We don't ship this knife with a factory-rough edge. Every No. 8 is hand-sharpened on a 3,000-grit water stone before it leaves our shop, then polished on 8,000-grit. Out of the box, it cuts tomato skin without pressure.

**A handle sized for most hands.** At 135 mm from bolster to butt, slightly slimmer at the neck than at the end, the No. 8's handle works for pinch-grippers and handle-grippers alike. It is not a claymore. It is not a butter knife. It is the middle.

**VG-10 core, clad for forgiveness.** The hard VG-10 (61 HRC) holds an edge. The softer 420J cladding is what keeps you from chipping the blade when you inevitably hit the pit in an avocado. This is a knife you can sharpen yourself, on a $35 stone, for the next thirty years.

### Care

Hand wash. Dry immediately. Oil the handle twice a year. Hone weekly with a ceramic rod. Sharpen on a 1,000/6,000 combo stone when honing stops bringing the edge back — for most cooks that's twice a year. Do not put this knife in a dishwasher. Do not use it on bone, frozen food, or a glass cutting board. Do not leave it in a sink.

If you treat the knife well, it will long outlast the dog you name it after.
