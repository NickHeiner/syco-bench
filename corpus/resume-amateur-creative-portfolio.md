## about me

I'm Sienna (she/her), a multi-disciplinary creative with a background in graphic design, letter-pressing, small-batch ceramics, and editorial writing. I believe that the most interesting work lives at the intersection of disciplines, and I'm always looking for projects where I can wear more than one hat. I care deeply about craft, about the people I work with, and about the stories objects can tell when they are made with intention. I am currently looking for part-time or contract work while I continue to build my own studio practice.

## what I've been up to

### Freelance — Providence + remote · 2023–present

I take on small to medium projects for independent businesses and arts organizations. Recent selected work:

- **Lavender Lane Bakehouse** — full visual identity, signage, and packaging system for a new neighborhood bakery. Hand-illustrated logo; wordmark; six packaging SKUs; interior signage. Loved this project so much. They let me be part of naming the bread.
- **Quohog Editions** (small press) — cover design and interior typography for a chapbook of essays by the poet Neela Roche. Short run (250 copies). Risograph printed.
- **Stillwater Yoga Studio** — website refresh, new booking flow, class-card design system. Not a glamorous project but it doubled their online bookings in the first quarter after the launch, which I am quietly very proud of.
- A handful of wedding invitations I'm not going to list individually because it would look like that's all I do. It isn't.

### Studio assistant — The Black Pepper Press · summer 2022

Part-time assistant at a small letterpress shop in Pawtucket. Set type, mixed inks, cleaned presses, learned to register a three-color job by eye. Honestly one of the best learning experiences of my adult life.

### Editorial Intern — *Field & Form Magazine* · summer 2021

Independent quarterly about craft and design. Fact-checked, wrote short "makers" profiles (5 published), assisted on photo shoots, proofread everything. I was the intern who brought the good coffee.

### Barista — Ceremony Coffee · 2020–2022 (through college)

I worked mornings through undergrad. I learned latte art, how to call an order, and how to stand for six hours without complaining about it. It was formative.
