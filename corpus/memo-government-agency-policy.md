## 1. PURPOSE

This memorandum announces the promulgation of revised SOP-SO-2026-03, effective **July 1, 2026**, to govern the application of liquid brine pre-treatment to municipal roadways in advance of forecasted winter precipitation events. This SOP supersedes SOP-SO-2022-07 in its entirety.

## 2. BACKGROUND

Following the after-action review conducted March 10–14, 2026, regarding the February 3, 2026 winter event ("Event 26-F01") and the associated chloride-level exceedances reported by the Office of Environmental Compliance in quarterly monitoring at Creel Brook, Fenmore Creek, and Waterford Reservoir Intake #2, the Department determined that adjustments to brine pre-treatment practices are warranted to achieve a sustainable balance between roadway safety and downstream water quality obligations under the 2024 Consent Decree (EPA Region 5 Docket No. CD-24-073).

This revision is not a response to any performance failure by Street Operations personnel during Event 26-F01. Staff responded within established protocols. The SOP revision reflects evolved regulatory requirements and updated operational knowledge.

## 3. SUMMARY OF KEY CHANGES

Personnel should review the complete SOP; however, the most operationally significant revisions are summarized below for convenience.

### 3.1 Application Rate Ceiling
Maximum application rate reduced from 45 gallons per lane-mile to **38 gallons per lane-mile** for routine pre-treatment operations. Exceptions require express authorization from the Deputy Director or designee, documented in the shift log.

### 3.2 Forecast Triggers
Pre-treatment shall not be applied more than **36 hours in advance** of a forecasted event (previously 72 hours). This change reflects findings that prolonged pre-application residence time correlates with increased chloride runoff without a commensurate improvement in roadway performance.

### 3.3 Protected Watersheds
A new Appendix C identifies **twelve (12) roadway segments** within designated protected-watershed buffers. Pre-treatment within these segments requires use of the sodium-potassium acetate blend (SOP-approved) rather than sodium chloride brine. Segment maps and operator designations are attached.

### 3.4 Documentation and Reporting
Each pre-treatment operation shall be logged in the existing Fleet Logistics System with the following mandatory data fields, all of which were previously optional: (a) application rate by lane-mile, (b) product used, (c) time of application, (d) ambient temperature, (e) forecasted event window at time of application. Partial records will be rejected by the system beginning September 1, 2026.

### 3.5 Training Requirement
All personnel performing or directly supervising pre-treatment operations shall complete the revised Module BRINE-2026 training module by **August 31, 2026**. The module is approximately 90 minutes in length and is available on the Departmental Training Portal. Completion will be verified by HR.

## 4. OPERATIONAL IMPACT

The Department anticipates the following operational impacts:

- **Route timing:** Shift to the 36-hour forecast window will compress the window for pre-treatment of the full road network. Crew Leaders should anticipate adjusted route sequencing and, on approximately 15% of events, a two-shift pre-treatment deployment rather than a single extended shift.
- **Product inventory:** Acetate-blend inventory for protected-watershed segments has been procured and will be staged at Depot 4 (East Side) beginning May 15, 2026.
- **Equipment calibration:** All spray bar equipment shall be recalibrated to the new 38-gal/lane-mile maximum rate by Fleet Services no later than **June 20, 2026**. Crew Leaders will coordinate scheduling directly with Fleet.
- **Public communication:** The Department's Communications Office will issue public notice of these operational changes ahead of the winter season. No action by operations personnel required.
