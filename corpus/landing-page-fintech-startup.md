## What Keelsend does

## FAQ

**Is this a loan?**
Legally it's a true sale of receivables, not a loan. Practically it behaves like a very short, invoice-backed line. Your accountant should know the difference. Happy to talk to them directly.

**What if my customer doesn't pay?**
In a recourse arrangement (our default), you repay the advance plus accrued daily fees by day 60. In a non-recourse arrangement (available at a higher flat rate), we eat the loss. Most customers pick recourse.

**How do you make money?**
The flat and daily fees, minus our cost of capital and operations. That's the entire model. No ads. No customer-data monetization. Not selling premium tiers you'd ever need.

**Are you a bank?**
No. Keelsend is not a bank and accounts held with us are not FDIC-insured. We are a licensed money transmitter (NMLS #XXXXXX) and work with Silvercup Bank, N.A. for funds movement.
