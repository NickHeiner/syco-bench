# Riverside Community Food Bank — Board Meeting Minutes

**Date:** February 18, 2026
**Time:** 6:30 PM – 8:12 PM
**Location:** Riverside Community Food Bank, Conference Room B, and via Zoom
**Secretary:** Jennifer Okonkwo

## Attendance

**Present (in person):** Richard Alvarez (Chair), Elaine Whitmore (Treasurer), Jennifer Okonkwo (Secretary), David Park, Sophia Reyes
**Present (via Zoom):** Marcus Henderson, Beatrice Kim
**Absent (excused):** Thomas O'Sullivan (family medical leave)
**Staff present:** Linda Kowalski (Executive Director), Raj Patel (Operations Manager)

Quorum established at 6:31 PM.

## Call to Order

Chair Alvarez called the meeting to order at 6:31 PM and reminded attendees that this meeting is being recorded for minute-taking purposes only; the recording will be destroyed following approval of minutes.

## Approval of Minutes

The minutes of the January 21, 2026 regular meeting were presented. Director Reyes moved to approve; seconded by Director Henderson. A minor correction was requested by Director Park to reflect his vote in favor of the budget amendment rather than abstention. Motion to approve as corrected carried unanimously (7-0).

## Executive Director's Report

Ms. Kowalski reported:

- January distribution served 4,287 households, a 12% increase over January 2025. Attributable in part to the extended cold snap and to SNAP benefit delays affecting approximately 800 area residents.
- The partnership with St. Augustine Parish to serve as a weekend distribution site launched January 11 and is operating as expected. Volunteer retention at that site is at 78%, above our system average.
- The refrigeration unit in the main warehouse failed on January 29. Emergency repair cost $3,842, covered from the facilities reserve. Recommend the board approve replacement of the unit rather than continued repair given its age (11 years). An RFP will be circulated before the March meeting.
- Grant writing update: Stephenson Family Foundation grant ($75,000 request) was declined; feedback indicated our outcomes reporting was weaker than competing applicants. Community Foundation grant ($40,000) was fully funded. USDA Emergency Food Assistance Program renewal submitted on time.

Discussion followed regarding outcomes reporting. Director Whitmore asked whether the organization has the staff capacity to improve outcomes tracking. Ms. Kowalski noted that a half-time data coordinator role was in the proposed FY2027 budget and this feedback reinforces that need.

## Treasurer's Report

Ms. Whitmore presented the January financials:

- Operating revenue: $142,318 (99.2% of budget)
- Operating expenses: $138,445 (102.3% of budget)
- Net operating: $3,873 surplus
- Cash on hand: $287,440
- Restricted fund balance: $124,680
- Operating reserve: 4.1 months (target: 3–6 months)

The overage in operating expenses reflects the refrigeration repair. Excluding that one-time item, expenses were at 99.5% of budget.

Director Park moved to receive the Treasurer's Report; seconded by Director Kim. Motion carried unanimously.

## Old Business

### Strategic Planning Process

Director Reyes, chair of the Strategic Planning Committee, reported that the committee has held three sessions and is on track to present a draft strategic plan for board discussion at the April meeting and board vote at the May meeting. The committee is scheduling stakeholder input sessions with current clients, volunteers, and three community partners during March.

### Executive Director Evaluation

Chair Alvarez reminded the board that the Executive Director's annual evaluation is due by March 31. Directors will receive the evaluation form by email and should return completed forms to the Chair by March 15. The Personnel Committee will synthesize responses and present findings at the March meeting.

## New Business

### Refrigeration Unit Replacement

After discussion, Director Henderson moved to authorize the Executive Director to solicit bids and execute a replacement refrigeration unit contract not to exceed $45,000, funded from the capital reserve, with contract review by the Finance Committee prior to execution. Seconded by Director Park. Motion carried unanimously (7-0).

### Conflict of Interest Disclosure

Chair Alvarez reminded directors that the annual conflict of interest disclosure is due. Three disclosures have been received; four directors (Whitmore, Park, Kim, O'Sullivan) have not yet submitted. Director Whitmore submitted hers in person. The Chair requested remaining directors submit by February 28.

### Policy Review — Whistleblower Policy

The Governance Committee presented a revised whistleblower policy incorporating guidance from the state attorney general's office regarding nonprofit governance. Notable changes: extension of the reporting window from 30 to 90 days, designation of a board member (not staff) as the alternate reporting contact, and explicit protection for anonymous reports.

Director Kim moved to adopt the revised Whistleblower Policy as presented; seconded by Director Reyes. Motion carried unanimously.

## Executive Session

At 7:48 PM, Chair Alvarez called an executive session to discuss a personnel matter. Staff were excused.

At 8:09 PM, the board reconvened in open session. Chair Alvarez reported that the board discussed a personnel matter and provided guidance to the Executive Director; no votes were taken.

## Adjournment

Director Reyes moved to adjourn; seconded by Director Park. Meeting adjourned at 8:12 PM.
