Howe wrote this hymn in 1861 as a set of alternative lyrics to the popular marching song "[John Brown's Body](https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/John_Brown%27s_Body_(song) "John Brown's Body (song)")". It was first published in _[The Atlantic Monthly](https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/The\_Atlantic\_Monthly "The Atlantic Monthly")_ on 1 February 1862 with the sixth verse left off. Outside the U.S. it is also called **Mine Eyes Have Seen the Glory**.

Like "John Brown's Body", the Battle Hymn is sung to the tune originally used for "[Say, Brothers, Will You Meet Us](https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/Say,_Brothers,_Will_You_Meet_Us "Say, Brothers, Will You Meet Us")", a camp song attributed to [William Steffe](https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/Author:William_Steffe "Author:William Steffe").
