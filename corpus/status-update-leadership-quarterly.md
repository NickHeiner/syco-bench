## Context

Q1 was our first full quarter operating under the reorganized Customer Operations org (merger of Customer Success and Support completed Dec 15). The team is now 94 people across four sub-teams: Onboarding, Mid-Market Success, Enterprise Success, and Support. We exited Q4 2025 with two lagging metrics (onboarding-completion rate and Tier-1 ticket backlog) and one strong metric (enterprise NPS). The Q1 plan was designed to move the two lagging metrics without sacrificing the strong one.

## Progress on the three priorities

### Priority 1: Fix onboarding completion

- Stood up the Onboarding sub-team (12 people) on Jan 8 with Aarav Iyer as lead. Migrated off the legacy onboarding-script model to milestone-based coaching.
- Shipped the redesigned onboarding dashboard (in-product) on Feb 18. Early data suggests a ~7-point lift in 30-day completion directly attributable.
- Introduced weekly cohort reviews. Aarav has been tight with the data — my read is this team has found its footing faster than I expected.

### Priority 2: Burn down support backlog and move median resolution under 24 hours

- Deployed the priority-triage retraining for all 31 Tier-1 reps in January. Average first-touch accuracy (judged by QA audit) went from 74 percent to 87 percent.
- Began using the new agent-assist tool in February. Initial skepticism in the team; by end of March, voluntary usage was 81 percent.
- We are below the 24-hour median target and the backlog is under 400. I want to flag that some of the backlog reduction reflects tickets we consciously deflected to self-service — genuine, not a gaming-the-metric move, but worth watching that we do not trade ticket count for CSAT decline (we haven't yet; CSAT is flat).

### Priority 3: Protect enterprise NPS while two other sub-teams churn

- Enterprise NPS dipped 3 points. Drivers in the qualitative comments: two named accounts cited delays on their custom-integration requests, and one cited frustration with our Q1 pricing changes for add-ons. Neither is a Customer Operations-owned driver, but both land on our plate.
- Mitigation plan for Q2: quarterly business reviews for the top 40 accounts moving from semi-annual to quarterly cadence; two dedicated integration-engineering hours per named account per month (agreed with Arjun in Platform Eng).

## What went better than expected

- Engagement pulse bounced faster than I planned for — I thought the reorg would depress it for two quarters. It did not.
- Expansion ARR came in $500K above plan. Most of this is the Mid-Market Success team getting better at upgrade conversations earlier in the renewal cycle.

## What went worse than expected

- Mid-market GRR missed plan by 0.7 points. Not catastrophic, but notable. Root cause looks like two large accounts that churned for reasons that were visible in health-score data 90 days out — we saw the signal, we did not act on it fast enough. Addressing in Q2 via the churn-save review process.

## Risks I am watching for Q2

1. *Support agent-assist tool pricing.* The vendor renewal in June includes a ~40 percent price increase. We need to decide by end of April whether to renew, negotiate, or evaluate alternatives. I will send a separate memo next week.
2. *Enterprise NPS leading indicator.* The 3-point decline is within noise, but if Q2 shows another decline we need to escalate.
3. *Capacity for the new Onboarding sub-team.* Aarav is running hot. I want to hire one more onboarding specialist in Q2 — requesting backfill slot #47 from the Q2 plan.
