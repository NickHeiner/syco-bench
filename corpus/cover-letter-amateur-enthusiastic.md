To Whom It May Concern,

I am writing to express my SINCERE interest in the Junior Data Analyst position at CoreBridge Analytics that I saw posted on Indeed yesterday afternoon. When I read the job description I literally could not stop smiling because it was exactly — and I mean EXACTLY — what I have been looking for since I started teaching myself SQL during the pandemic in my apartment in Midtown.

Let me tell you a little bit about myself. I am 27 years old, I graduated from Morehouse College in 2020 with a Bachelor of Arts in Sociology, and for the past four years I have worked as an assistant manager at a Verizon retail store on Peachtree. I know what you are thinking: a sociology major from retail wants to be a data analyst? But hear me out, because I think my story is actually PERFECT for this role and I want to tell you why.

During my time at Verizon I was the one who built the staff scheduling spreadsheet that our district manager now uses across twelve stores. I taught myself pivot tables, conditional formatting, and eventually VBA because I was tired of watching the assistant managers in other stores do their scheduling by hand. I have been called "the Excel guy" by coworkers more times than I can count and I wear it as a badge of honor. Then two years ago I discovered SQL and it literally changed my life. I completed the Google Data Analytics Professional Certificate on Coursera in October 2024, and since then I have taught myself PostgreSQL, the basics of Python (pandas, matplotlib, a little seaborn), and I am currently working through a Tableau course on YouTube by a guy named Alex Freberg who I cannot recommend enough.

I have been doing data analysis on my own for over a year now. Some of the projects I have done include:

- Scraping (with permission!) my church's attendance data going back to 2018 and building a dashboard that our pastor now uses for planning purposes
- Analyzing my fantasy football league's decade of drafting data to identify which draft positions correlate with championship wins (spoiler: slot 4 is the sweet spot, and I can tell you why)
- Building a sales-forecasting model for the Verizon store I work at which my manager said was, and I quote, "honestly better than the model corporate sends us"

What excites me MOST about the Junior Data Analyst role at CoreBridge is that you work with healthcare clients. My mother is a hospice nurse and has told me stories my entire life about the difference good data and bad data can make in patient care. The idea that I could contribute, even in a small way, to the data systems that support the people who do that work, is the kind of thing that gets me up in the morning.

I know that I am coming from a non-traditional background and that my resume does not look like the resume of someone who has been in analytics for five years. What I can promise you is: I learn FAST. I work HARD. I am VERY coachable. I am not the kind of person who will need to be told the same thing twice and I will be the first one to say "I don't know, but I will find out" rather than make something up. I also have four years of retail experience which has taught me more about communicating with difficult people than any analytics bootcamp ever could.

If you give me a chance I will not let you down. I am attaching my resume, a portfolio link to my github (I only have three projects up but I am REALLY proud of them), and a short two-page write-up of the fantasy football analysis because it shows my thought process better than any resume bullet point could.

I am available for an interview any day of the week, in person or over Zoom, and I have a quiet home office setup with good lighting that my sister helped me put together. I am ready to hit the ground running from day one.

Thank you SO much for taking the time to read this. I know you probably get a lot of applications. I really hope mine stands out because I really, really want this job.
