Dear Dr. Haight,

I am writing to express my interest in the Chief Operating Officer role at Meridian Health Partners, following my conversation with Chandra Pillai last month and her kind suggestion that I reach out to you directly.

Over the past twenty-two years I have held operating leadership roles at three integrated delivery systems, most recently as Senior Vice President of Operations at Cascade Mountain Health, where I lead service-line strategy and performance across eleven hospitals and ninety-four ambulatory sites in Washington and northern Idaho. What has kept me in this field — through the ACA rollout, through two mergers, through the pandemic, and through the margin compression of the last three years — is a conviction that operational excellence is ultimately a clinical concern. A smoother surgical throughput is not just a financial win; it is a patient going home a day earlier to sleep in her own bed.

I was drawn to Meridian for three reasons. First, your board's public commitment to maintain the Adelanto and Montrose rural facilities, even as peer systems divest, reflects a mission clarity that I want to help operationalize. Second, the clinical integration work Dr. Samuel Tenorio described at the AHA Rural Health Forum was genuinely impressive — I came back from that panel and asked my team to study what you had done on sepsis-bundle compliance. Third, the COO role as scoped includes direct accountability for the quality and safety organization, which I believe is the correct structure; separating operations from safety is an artifact of older org charts that I have seen cause real harm.

If selected, my first ninety days would focus on three priorities: ground-truthing the current operational scorecard against what frontline leaders actually use to run their units, revisiting the capital allocation model for facility modernization given current interest-rate realities, and building a direct-report-to-CEO version of the weekly operational review that I have found invaluable at Cascade Mountain. I would do this in close partnership with CEO Marcus Delacroix, whose recent remarks about "not confusing motion with progress" resonated strongly.

I recognize that the board is evaluating internal and external candidates for this role, and I have deep respect for the strength of Meridian's existing leadership. If I can be useful in any capacity — including as a reference for your existing search — I am glad to be so.

My CV is enclosed. I would welcome a conversation at your convenience.

Respectfully,

Jonathan W. Alvarez
