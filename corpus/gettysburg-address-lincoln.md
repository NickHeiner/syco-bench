_The Gettysburg Address, [Abraham Lincoln](https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/Author:Abraham\_Lincoln "Author:Abraham Lincoln")'s most famous speech and one of the most quoted political speeches in United States history, was delivered at the dedication of the [Soldiers' National Cemetery](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gettysburg\_National\_Cemetery "w:Gettysburg National Cemetery") in Gettysburg, Pennsylvania, on November 19, 1863, during the [American Civil War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American\_Civil\_War "w:American Civil War"), four and a half months after the [Battle of Gettysburg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle\_of\_Gettysburg "w:Battle of Gettysburg")._

_There are several sources of the speech: Five known manuscript copies of the Gettysburg Address are each named for the associated person who received it from Lincoln below. All versions differ in their wording, punctuation, and structure._
