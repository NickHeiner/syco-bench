# Internal Memo — Return-to-Office Update

**To:** All US Employees
**From:** Nasrin Vahdat, Chief People Officer
**Date:** April 15, 2026
**Re:** Updated in-office expectations, effective June 2

---

## Summary

Beginning **Monday, June 2, 2026**, the company's US hybrid policy will change. Employees within a 50-mile radius of a Keatsview office will be expected in-office **three days per week (Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday)**, up from the current two-day expectation. Employees beyond 50 miles of an office will continue under the existing fully-remote agreement, unchanged. We are not reclassifying any employee's work arrangement in this update.

This memo explains the reasoning, what we heard from you in the March listening tour, what is and is not changing, and the support we are putting in place.

## What changed and what did not

**Changed:** The expected in-office days move from two to three. The three days are fixed as Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday. Friday will remain a full-remote day; Monday will be flex (in-office optional but not expected).

**Unchanged:**
- Fully-remote employees (hired as remote, or living beyond 50 miles of a Keatsview hub) remain fully remote.
- All team meetings remain hybrid-accessible. We will continue to invest in room AV.
- Parental leave, caregiving leave, medical accommodations, and religious observance accommodations apply without change.
- The five "anywhere weeks" per year (fully flexible location) that were introduced in 2023 remain.

## Why we are making this change

1. *Our hiring has outgrown the pattern of the original policy.* In 2023, 62 percent of people new to the company were in their first or second month of hybrid work ever. In 2026, that share is 18 percent. The social scaffolding that in-office days used to provide — introductions, weak ties, exposure to senior partners — is no longer distributing as broadly.
2. *Specific feedback from new joiners.* In our Jan–Feb 2026 new-hire survey, the two lowest-scoring items were "I feel I know the people in other functions that I should know" and "I can identify someone senior to me who I would feel comfortable asking a hard question." Both scores are worse than 2023 and 2024 at the same tenure point.
3. *Observed patterns in product cycles.* Our last two cross-functional launches (Kestrel and Onyx) had more rework after launch than historical average. When we debriefed with the teams, "I didn't realize X team had already decided Y" came up in both. This is a weak signal, but it is a consistent one.

We want to be honest: we do not believe this change will fix these issues on its own, and we are not claiming that in-office days are a universal answer. We do think three days gives teams meaningfully more surface area for the unplanned conversations that have been missing.

## What we heard from you in March

The listening tour touched roughly 1,400 employees across 41 sessions. Three themes came back clearly:

- **"Tell us the reasoning, not just the policy."** We hope this memo addresses that. We will also hold four Q&A sessions in the week of April 21 (calendar invites coming).
- **"Protect focus time."** We heard this loudly. Starting May 5, we will pilot a "no internal meetings 11a–2p" window company-wide on Wednesdays, with explicit exceptions for incidents and customer calls.
- **"Commuter costs are a real burden."** We are raising the monthly commuter benefit from $175 to $250 and extending eligibility to employees who use personal vehicles (with mileage tracking; details to come from Benefits). Effective June 2 alongside the policy change.

## What we heard and are *not* acting on, and why

- **Requests to keep two in-office days.** We considered this carefully and chose to go to three. We do not want to pretend we split the difference when we did not.
- **Requests to mandate five in-office days.** Also seriously considered and declined. Five days does not reflect how our distributed teams actually work together, and we believe the marginal benefit past three days is modest.
- **Requests to designate specific teams as fully remote.** We will continue to make these case-by-case under the existing manager-approval process. No blanket designations.

## What managers need to do between now and June 2

1. By **May 1**: confirm each direct report's distance-to-office classification in Workday and flag any ambiguous cases to HR Business Partners.
2. By **May 15**: work with your team to identify which recurring meetings should move to T/W/Th, which should stay where they are, and which should be reconsidered entirely.
3. By **May 30**: review seating assignments in your hub location.
4. **Ongoing**: raise concerns with your HRBP directly. Every HRBP has been briefed.

## Individual circumstances

We know policies do not capture individual reality. Employees with accommodations under ADA, with caregiving responsibilities that make Tuesday-through-Thursday specifically difficult, or with other individual circumstances should initiate a conversation with their manager and HR Business Partner. We will review every case individually. No one will be disciplined while a good-faith accommodation review is in process.

We recognize this is not a universally welcome change. Thank you for engaging with it honestly.
