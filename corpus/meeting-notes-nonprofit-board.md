## 1. Call to Order and Quorum (6:07 p.m.)

Chair Kasprzak called the meeting to order at 6:07 p.m. and confirmed quorum present (9 of 10 voting members).

## 2. Approval of Minutes from December 12, 2025 Meeting

**MOTION** by D. Oyeniran, seconded by L. Sokolov, to approve the December 12, 2025 minutes as circulated.
**Discussion:** None.
**Vote:** 9 in favor, 0 opposed, 0 abstaining. **Motion carried.**

## 3. Executive Director's Report (Dr. Weissmuller)

- Program enrollment as of March 15: 412 adult learners (Q1 2026 target was 380; +8%). Waitlist at 47 is the highest we have had.
- Staffing: one new part-time tutor coordinator hired (Aisha Pemberton, starts April 6). We are actively recruiting for the open volunteer coordinator role; two candidates in final interviews.
- Grants: the Bettendorf Family Foundation general operating grant ($75,000, two-year) was awarded March 1. The Dunnell Trust application ($150,000 over three years) was declined February 28. Staff will not reapply this cycle but will revisit for the 2027 cycle.
- Facility: the ventilation repair at the Lincoln Park satellite site is complete. Cost came in at $11,840, under the approved $14,000 budget.
- Program concern: the GED preparation cohort that launched in January is retaining 22% of enrolled learners at the ten-week mark, below the 40% target. Staff is reviewing the curriculum with the provider and will report back at the June meeting. Trustees asked whether this warrants pausing a planned September cohort; staff recommends waiting for the full 16-week data.

**Discussion:** G. Petersen asked whether the waitlist suggests a need for expanded capacity or whether it reflects a seasonal bump. Dr. Weissmuller responded that enrollment is genuinely up year-over-year and the waitlist is not fully explained by seasonality. Board asked staff to bring a capacity-expansion analysis for discussion at the June meeting.

## 4. Treasurer's Report (T. Halliburton)

- Q1 2026 revenue: $328,400 (vs. budget $296,000). Favorable variance driven primarily by the Bettendorf grant receipt in Q1 rather than Q2 as budgeted.
- Q1 2026 expenses: $271,900 (vs. budget $278,000). Favorable, primarily due to the ventilation repair coming in under budget and a delayed hire.
- Operating reserve: $412,000, equivalent to 5.1 months of operating expenses (policy target: 4–6 months).
- Investment portfolio: up 2.1% YTD; holdings unchanged.
- Outstanding payables are current. Outstanding receivables total $18,400, all from pledged but not-yet-received individual gifts; none past 90 days.

**MOTION** by K. Alcantara, seconded by M. Brennan, to accept the Treasurer's Report.
**Vote:** 9 in favor, 0 opposed. **Motion carried.**

## 7. Strategic Plan Progress (Dr. Weissmuller + Chair)

Year-two milestones from the 2024–2028 Strategic Plan:

- *Expand adult literacy enrollment to 500 by end of 2026.* Currently at 412; on track.
- *Launch GED preparation program.* Launched January 2026; retention concern noted under Item 3.
- *Formalize employer-partnership pipeline.* Two MOUs signed this quarter (SunRiver Hospitality, Northgrove Manufacturing). Target for year was three.
