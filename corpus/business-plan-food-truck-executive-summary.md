# Business Plan — Executive Summary: "Southside Birria Co." Food Truck

**Prepared by:** Carlos E. Ramírez-Hollister, Founder
**Date:** March 2026
**For:** Route 29 Community Lenders and the Small Business Administration Microloan Program
**Requested funding:** $48,500 (combined microloan + personal capital)

---

## 1. Business Overview

Southside Birria Co. will be a fixed-route food truck specializing in slow-braised beef birria tacos, quesabirria, and consommé, served from a 24-foot commissary-equipped truck operating a rotating schedule across four verified-demand locations in the Richmond, Virginia metropolitan area. Legal structure: Virginia LLC, single-member, already formed (March 4, 2026).

The concept is narrow by design. We will open with a five-item menu — three taco variants, one quesabirria, and one combo plate — plus two non-alcoholic drinks. We will deliberately not compete on menu breadth; we will compete on doing one regional tradition carefully, at a price point Richmond's office-worker and late-lunch crowd can sustain twice a week.

## 2. The Founder

Carlos Ramírez-Hollister has nine years of kitchen experience, most recently four years as sous chef at Finca Azul in Charlottesville (Jefferson Cup semifinalist, 2023) and three years as line cook at Nota Bene in Washington, DC. He holds ServSafe Manager certification, has completed the Richmond Food Truck Association's 14-week "Starting on Wheels" cohort (graduated January 2026), and co-wrote the back-of-house SOPs currently in use at Finca Azul.

Carlos grew up in Norfolk; his mother is from Tijuana and taught him the base recipe for the birria that will anchor the menu. This is a personal project as much as a commercial one.

## 3. The Opportunity

Richmond has a thriving food-truck scene (approximately 130 active trucks per the Richmond Food Truck Association's 2025 registry), but only three trucks currently serve birria, and none of them run a fixed-route schedule that allows a regular customer to know where they will be on a given weekday. Our primary research (a six-week on-site survey at four candidate locations, 1,112 intercepts) found:

- 68 percent of respondents had eaten birria at least once;
- 41 percent said they would buy from a consistent weekday truck "once a week or more";
- average willingness-to-pay on a three-taco plate was $14.60, slightly above our planned $13.50 entry point.

Wednesdays and Fridays are operationally budgeted as prep/catering days. Sundays closed.

**Requested loan:** $38,500 through the SBA Microloan Program via Route 29 Community Lenders. **Founder equity:** $10,000 from personal savings.

**Projected Year 1 revenue:** $237,000. (Basis: 72 operating days Q2, 108 Q3–Q4; conservative estimate of 78 average plates per service day; Saturday evening volume modeled separately at 95 plates.)

**Projected Year 1 gross margin:** 62 percent.

**Projected Year 1 net income:** $17,200 (after loan servicing, owner's draw of $38,000, and depreciation).

Break-even analysis: truck reaches monthly break-even at approximately 58 plates per service day, achieved by Month 3 in the base case.

## 7. Risks and Mitigation

- *Weather and seasonality.* Richmond food-truck revenue historically declines 35–45 percent in January and February. Mitigated by a catering-first strategy Q1 2027, already in early conversation with two corporate accounts.
- *Permit risk at Manchester Commons.* Application in review; fallback anchor location identified at West Broad Market (verbal expression of interest, not yet formal).
- *Sole-operator concentration.* Founder is currently the only line cook. Co-cook candidate identified (former Finca Azul colleague), intended hire at Month 4 subject to revenue.
- *Equipment reliability on a used truck.* Pre-purchase inspection passed but the truck has 148,000 miles. Maintenance reserve of $200/month included in the financial model. Breakdown insurance carried.

## 8. Why This Loan, Now

We have prepared for 14 months, validated demand on foot, secured two location commitments, and held the launch off the calendar twice to stay within budget. The truck is in our possession and inspected; the commissary is reserved; the first location is confirmed. The $38,500 microloan closes the gap between founder capital and a working operating buffer. Without that buffer, a single two-week breakdown in Month 2 could end the business. With it, we believe we have a very good chance at self-sustaining operations by month six.
