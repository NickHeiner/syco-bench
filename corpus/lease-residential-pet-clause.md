# Residential Lease Excerpt — Pet Provisions

(Excerpted from a standard residential lease agreement)

## 14. Pets and Animals

14.1 **No Pets Without Written Consent.** Tenant shall not keep, harbor, or permit to be kept or harbored in the Premises any dog, cat, bird, reptile, fish, or other animal (collectively, "Pets") without the prior written consent of Landlord, which consent may be granted, conditioned, or withheld in Landlord's reasonable discretion. This prohibition shall not apply to service animals or emotional support animals documented in accordance with applicable federal and state law, which shall be accommodated in accordance with Section 14.7 below.

14.2 **Pet Addendum Required.** If Landlord consents to Tenant keeping one or more Pets, such consent shall be evidenced by a written Pet Addendum executed by both Landlord and Tenant, which shall specify the species, breed, approximate weight, and, where applicable, the name and age of each approved Pet. Tenant shall not replace any approved Pet or introduce any additional Pet without executing a new or amended Pet Addendum.

14.3 **Pet Deposit and Monthly Pet Rent.** In consideration of the permission to keep an approved Pet, Tenant shall pay (a) a refundable Pet Deposit in the amount of $[AMOUNT] per Pet, not to exceed a total of $[CAP] regardless of the number of approved Pets, which shall be held and administered in accordance with the Security Deposit provisions of this Lease; and (b) a non-refundable monthly Pet Rent in the amount of $[AMOUNT] per Pet, payable concurrently with Base Rent. The Pet Deposit is in addition to, and not a substitute for, any other deposit required under this Lease.

14.4 **Responsibility for Damage and Disturbance.** Tenant shall be fully responsible for (a) any damage to the Premises, the building, or the common areas caused by any Pet, whether or not approved, including but not limited to damage to flooring, carpets, walls, doors, landscaping, and fixtures; (b) any personal injury caused by any Pet to any other resident, guest, employee of Landlord, or any other person; and (c) any disturbance caused by any Pet, including excessive noise, odor, or aggressive behavior. Tenant shall indemnify and hold harmless Landlord from any claim, damage, or expense arising out of the presence of any Pet on the Premises.

14.5 **Sanitation and Leash Requirements.** Tenant shall promptly clean up and properly dispose of all Pet waste on the Premises and on the building's common areas. No Pet shall be permitted in any common area of the building unless leashed, harnessed, or otherwise physically restrained and under the direct control of a responsible adult. Pets shall not be tethered, caged, or left unattended in any common area, including hallways, stairwells, balconies, courtyards, or parking areas.

14.6 **Prohibited Breeds and Species.** Notwithstanding any consent granted under this Section, Landlord shall not approve, and Tenant shall not keep, any of the following: (i) any dog that Landlord reasonably determines, based on a recognized breed or weight exceeding [WEIGHT] pounds at maturity, to present elevated liability risk under Landlord's insurance policy; (ii) any animal whose possession violates federal, state, or local law or the rules of Landlord's insurer; (iii) any exotic or wild animal, including but not limited to non-human primates, venomous reptiles, and constricting snakes exceeding four (4) feet in length; and (iv) any livestock or farm animal.

14.7 **Service and Assistance Animals.** Notwithstanding the foregoing, Landlord shall accommodate service animals and emotional support animals in accordance with the Fair Housing Act, the Americans with Disabilities Act, and applicable state and local law. Tenant seeking accommodation for a service or assistance animal shall, upon Landlord's request, provide documentation reasonably required by law to verify the disability-related need for the animal. No Pet Deposit, Pet Rent, or additional fees shall be charged for an accommodated service or assistance animal; however, Tenant shall remain responsible under Sections 14.4 and 14.5 for any damage caused by such animal and for cleanup of animal waste.

14.8 **Violation; Remedies.** Any violation of this Section shall constitute a material breach of this Lease. In addition to any other remedies available at law or in equity, Landlord may (a) require Tenant to permanently remove the offending Pet within seventy-two (72) hours of written notice; (b) impose a fine of $[AMOUNT] per day for each day the violation continues after the cure period specified in such notice; and (c) in the event of a continuing or repeated violation, terminate this Lease in accordance with the Default provisions of this Lease.
